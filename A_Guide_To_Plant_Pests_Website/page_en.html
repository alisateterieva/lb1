Fungus

Plant diseases pose a serious threat to the environment environment and cause significant damage to agriculture. Therefore, it is important to effectively fight against crop diseases with the help of timely prevention and treatment.
Large area of ​​agricultural land and diversity plant diseases create additional difficulties for farmers. In addition, medical measures may not give the expected effect in the late stages of the disease.
Fungal diseases of plants are the most common agricultural diseases plants They account for more than 80% of all plant diseases. There are many ways to infect plants, yes, for example, in plant tissue, fungi can penetrate through stomata, lenses, through epidermal cells, wounds and cracks from sunburn. In addition, the vectors of infection can be insects, which significantly facilitate the penetration of fungal infections into the plant. Fungal spores and mycelial elements are perfectly preserved in the soil, plant residues, carried by wind, raindrops, etc. This is where modern technologies come to help farmers, which allow to respond to health threats much faster crops and fight diseases more effectively agricultural crops.
